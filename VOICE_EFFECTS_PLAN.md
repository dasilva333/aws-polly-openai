# Voice Tuning & SSML Effects Plan

This document outlines the current state and future phases for enhancing the AWS Polly Neural voice (Ivy) using Speech Synthesis Markup Language (SSML).

## Current Baseline

The current voice generation uses Amazon Polly's Neural engine with the **Ivy** voice. Input text is sanitized (emojis removed) and converted to SSML.

**Existing Effects:**
*   `*text*` (Asterisks): Wraps narrative text in `<prosody rate="x-slow" volume="soft">` with dramatic pauses (`<break time="400ms"/>`) before and after.
*   `~` (Tilde): Injects a random "nyan" or "nyan nyan" with short pauses.

## Phase 1: Tuning and Emotion Enhancement

The goal of Phase 1 is to make the TTS output more expressive by reacting to standard punctuation commonly generated by roleplay/AI models.

**Planned Features:**
*   **Parentheses `(text)` or Brackets `[text]`:** Treat as inner thoughts or mutters. Shift pitch down and reduce volume (`x-soft`).
*   **Ellipsis `...`:** Treat as a dramatic silence or thoughtful pause. Replace with a longer `<break>` (e.g., 800ms).
*   **ALL CAPS:** Treat as yelling. Increase volume (`x-loud`) and pitch.
*   **Exclamation Points `! ` (at end of sentence):** Treat the preceding sentence as excited or forceful. Apply strong emphasis.
*   **Question Marks `? `:** Enhance the natural upward inflection of questions.
*   **Emoticon Handling (`0_0`):** Replace text-based emoticons that Polly literalizes (like `0_0`) with cute sounds/cues (e.g., "meow"). [COMPLETED]

## Phase 2: Advanced Formatting

Phase 2 focuses on more complex text structures and persona-based customization.

**Planned Features:**
*   **Persona Profiles:** Decouple rules into selectable characters (Catgirl, Bunnygirl, etc.) via `--profile`. [COMPLETED]
*   **Quoted Dialogue `"text"`:** Apply a subtle pitch shift to differentiate dialogue from internal monologue or narration.
*   **Stutters (`h-hello` or `I-I`):** Detect stutter patterns and replace hyphens with very short breaks to simulate a natural stumble.
*   **Verbal Tics (Future Ideas):** Randomly replacing/appending ending periods with character-specific tics (e.g., ". nya~") to add personality.

## Exploring Baseline Pitch & Rate

In addition to dynamic effects, we need to explore adjusting Ivy's baseline voice characteristics. The default speaking rate may be too slow for fast readers, and the timbre/pitch could be tweaked for a different feel.

**Exploration Goals:**
1.  **Rate Adjustments:** Test global rate increases (e.g., +10%, +15%) to find a comfortable listening speed that matches reading speed.
2.  **Pitch Adjustments:** Test global pitch shifts (e.g., +5% for slightly squeakier/younger, -5% for deeper/calmer) to find the ideal "character" voice.

A separate experimentation script should be created to easily generate samples with different baseline configurations.
